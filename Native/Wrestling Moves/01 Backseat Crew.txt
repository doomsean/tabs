==== Notes ====

Musically, to me, this feels very C# minor. The chords used are C#m (i), E (III), F#m (IV), A (VI),
and B (VII). Native are incredibly good at weaving together melodies with inversions created by the
two guitars and the bass. This song is a great example of that.

Guitar I is Edward (Ed?) O'Neill on the left and Guitar II is Daniel Evans on the right. They're
both using a fair bit of reverb and delay, and Edward in particular is using some kind of POG
(likely)  or Whammy-like effect for octave and pitch-shifting.

Live video footage used for corroboration/reference...

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hgZ4BoTXxTc
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JxHtNAVtUbc

-- Guitar II --

NOTE: Unless you've had a lot of practice with tapping, you will not be good at this at first. ;p
Keep your fingers in the same position as with the C#m7 chord; power chord shape with index,
middle, and ring fingers + pinky on the 7th fret of the top string. Leave your pinky planted on the
top string, but pull-off with your middle and ring fingers in opposition to the tapping. In other
words, when you tap-on with your right finger, pull-off with your left fingers (and vice versa).

h = hammer-on with left hand
t = tap with right hand
p = pluck with right hand
f = flick down with right hand

i = index
m = middle
r = ring
t = thumb

The top row beneath the line is how you attack the note(s); the second and third rows are the
fingers to use. Got it? Okay, let's go... ;p
