==== Notes ====

I haven't transcribed the acoustic guitar parts because, well... I don't like playing acoustic.
It doesn't sound difficult to figure out, however. Tune the guitar to drop C and put a capo on the
fourth fret, or drop D with a capo on the second fret. Obviously go with the former (drop C) if
you're playing it live on the same electric guitar used for the rest of the song.

The various electric guitar tones and effects are likewise fairly easy to discern. It sounds like
the same base crunch tone from the rest of the album, with occasional use of fuzz and manipulation
of the volumne knob on the guitar. Delay and reverb are used heavily throughout, and there might
be a bit of wah during the solos. Refer to the accompanying notes file for additional information.

Finally, upstrokes and downstrokes are noted in places where it may not be obvious. These generally
adhere to Nick DiSalvo's live performance (and what seems easiest to me).
