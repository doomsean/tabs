==== Notes ====

Guitar I is mostly double-tracked throughout the entire song, particularly when it noticeably
diverges from Guitar II. When the parts are roughly the same, it sounds like Guitar I maybe
_isn't_ double-tracked. It's a bit difficult to discern given the density of the recording.

The various guitar tones and effects aren't terribly difficult to figure out. It sounds like the
same base crunch tone from the rest of the album, with occasional use of fuzz and manipulation of
the volumne knob on the guitar. Delay and reverb are used heavily throughout. Refer to the
accompanying notes file for additional information.

Finally, upstrokes and downstrokes are noted in places where it may not be obvious. These generally
adhere to Nick DiSalvo's live performance (and what seems easiest to me).
