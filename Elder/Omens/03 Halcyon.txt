==== Notes ====

This track is musically very interesting in that it modulates between Bb minor (natural minor) and
Bb Dorian -- the difference being the 6th (minor 6th in natural minor vs. _major_ 6th in Dorian).
As such, in addition to seeing the different 6th notes in riffs and lead lines, there are also
instances of both Eb minor and Eb major chords/arpeggios. This is because the minor 6th is the
minor 3rd of an Eb minor chord, whereas a major 6th makes it an Eb _major_ chord.

Anyway, enough theory. It's one of my favorite tracks on Omens, and a lot of fun to play. :-)

Tonally speaking, Nick and Michael recently did a "Rig Rundown" with Premier Guitar in which they
describe the guitars, amps, and pedals used to record the album...

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5uFAFBF59vg

Also, for the most part, Guitar I is on the left (in the mix) and Guitar II is on the right.
Occasionally, however, one or the other is positioned more to the center.

Video footage used for corroboration/reference...

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jy0XghYMttI

==== [0:00] ====

NOTE: The intro seems very improvisational, and, given their krautrock, psychedelic, and jam band
influences, I suspect that Nick and Michael rarely play their respectives parts in the same way
every time. Nick (Guitar I) is playing Bbm arpeggios (occasionally suspending the 3rd) and scale
lines, while Michael is embellishing with spacey arpeggios and volume swells that often add various
other (natural minor and Dorian) scale tones.

In the spirit of improvisation, I'm not going to transcribe this part exactly as it's played on the
album. Rather, below are a few of the common patterns that appear. Any Bbm (or suspended) arpeggio
will sound good, though. Try experimenting with the other scale tones (2/9, 4, b6/6, b7) and in
various different parts of the neck.

The intro in the live video linked in the description above, in fact, differs significantly from
what's played on the recording.
